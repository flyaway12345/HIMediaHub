If you or someone you know is experiencing a mental health or substance use problem, there are several resources available to find out more information or get connected with help. Check out some mental health resources below: If you or someone you know is in crisis, contact the Suicide Prevention Lifeline at or dial 911 in case of emergency.
National Suicide Prevention Lifeline
Description: A 24/7 helpline providing free and confidential support for people in distress, including those experiencing suicidal thoughts.
Website: https://suicidepreventionlifeline.org/
Phone: DIAL 988
Crisis Text Line
Description: A free, 24/7 text-based crisis support service providing emotional assistance for those in crisis.
Website: https://www.crisistextline.org/
Text: Text "HOME" to 741741. You can also chat with a volunteer Crisis Counselor @ https://connect.crisistextline.org/chat
National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) Helpline
Description: NAMI offers information, support, and resources for individuals and families affected by mental health conditions.
Website: https://www.nami.org/
NAMI HelpLine is available M-F 10 am – 10 pm, ET.
Connect by phone 800-950-6264 or text "Helpline"
to 62640, or chat.
Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) Helpline
Description: SAMHSA provides referrals to local treatment facilities, support groups, and other resources for individuals and families facing mental health or substance abuse issues.
Website: https://www.samhsa.gov/
Phone: 1-800-662-HELP (1-800-662-4357)
Disaster Distress Helpline
The Disaster Distress Helpline (DDH) is the first national hotline dedicated to providing year-round disaster crisis counseling. This toll-free, multilingual, crisis support service is available 24/7 to all residents in the U.S. and its territories who are experiencing emotional distress related to natural or human-caused disasters.
Call or text 1-800-985-5990.
For Deaf and Hard of Hearing ASL Callers: To connect directly to an agent in American Sign Language, click the "ASL Now" button below or call 1-800-985-5990 from your videophone. ASL Support is available 24/7. FAQs for ASL NOW users.
https://vibrant.aslnow.io/app/8/10004

Veterans Crisis Line
Description: A helpline for U.S. military veterans in crisis, offering confidential support for issues related to mental health or readjustment difficulties after military service.
Website: https://www.veteranscrisisline.net/
Dial 988 and press 1, chat @ https://www.veteranscrisisline.net/get-help-now/chat/ or text 838255

National Domestic Abuse Hotline
Description: Provides support, resources, and safety planning for survivors of domestic violence.
Website: https://www.thehotline.org/
Phone: 1-800-799-SAFE (1-800-799-7233) CHAT: https://www.thehotline.org/ or text "START" to 88788. You can Click the red “X” in the upper-right corner or “Escape” button on your keyboard twice at any time to leave the site immediately
National Eating Disorders Association (NEDA) Helpline
Description: Offers support, resources, and treatment options for individuals struggling with eating disorders.
If you are in a crisis and need help immediately, call 988 or continue to contact Crisis Text Line by texting “NEDA” to 741741 to be connected with a trained volunteer at Crisis Text Line. Crisis Text Line is a separate organization staffed by volunteers who provide free, 24/7 support via text message to individuals who are struggling with mental health, including eating disorders, and are experiencing crisis situations.

SERVICES
https://www.findtreatment.gov/
FindTreatment is a website by SAMHSA, part of HHS. It helps find SUD and mental health treatment in the US. Users can search for facilities based on location, treatment type, and payment options. It offers resources for informed decisions on seeking help.
https://findsupport.gov/
Find Support is an online guide that helps people navigate through common questions when they are at the start of their journey to better behavioral health.
https://www.lgbtnearme.org/
Offers access to over 18,000 LGBTQIA+ community resources through-out the United States and Canada. From community centers, doctors & lawyers, social groups, sport teams, youth support and so much more!
